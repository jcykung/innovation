Eugenics

Breaking Down the Word

Eu-+genes

Eu-: A Greek prefix meaning "good" or "well." (e.g., Utopia, Euphoria).

Genes: From the Greek "genos," meaning "birth" or "origin."

So, "eugenics" literally means "well-born" or "good birth."

The Core Idea: Artificial Selection for Humans

Eugenics is a set of beliefs and practices that aims to improve the genetic quality of the human population. It applies the principles of selective breeding (like farming) to people. Supporters believed they could solve social problems by controlling who was allowed to have children.

Methods & Flaws

Eugenics was often presented as "science," but it was driven by social prejudice.

Negative Eugenics

Trying to reduce the number of "unfit" people. This included immigration bans, institutionalization, and forced sterilization (surgery to prevent pregnancy).

Positive Eugenics

Encouraging "fit" people (usually wealthy, white, and educated) to have more children through "Better Baby" contests and financial rewards.

The Fatal Flaw

Who decides what is "fit"? In the past, traits like poverty, criminal behaviour, low IQ scores, and even "immorality" were incorrectly blamed on genes. This allowed racism and classism to masquerade as biology.

Reading in Context: The Sexual Sterilization Act (Alberta)

In 1928, the province of Alberta passed the Sexual Sterilization Act. This law created a Eugenics Board that could approve the surgical sterilization of people deemed "mentally defective." The goal was to prevent them from passing their "bad genes" to children. Over the next 44 years, nearly 3,000 Albertans were sterilized.

One victim was Leilani Muir. In 1955, she was admitted to a training school for "mental defectives" simply because she was a neglected child who had scored low on a single IQ test. At age 14, she was told she was getting an appendectomy, but the doctors actually sterilized her without her knowledge. She only found out years later when she tried to start a family. In 1996, she successfully sued the government, proving she was never "mentally defective" and exposing the cruelty of the eugenics system.
