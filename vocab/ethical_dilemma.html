Ethical Dilemma

Breaking Down the Phrase: A Morphemic Analysis

Ethical&Dilemma

Ethical: Comes from the Greek word "ethos," which refers to a person's character, moral nature, or guiding beliefs. So, "ethical" means relating to moral principles.

Dilemma: Comes from the Greek words "di-" (meaning two) and "lemma" (meaning premise or proposition). It describes a situation where you have to choose between two competing options.

So, an "ethical dilemma" is a choice between two options, both of which conflict with your moral principles.

What is an Ethical Dilemma?

An ethical dilemma isn't just a difficult choice. It's a situation where two or more of your core values or moral principles are in conflict. To follow one value, you must violate another. There is no perfect "right" answer that satisfies all your moral obligations.

Key Idea: No Easy Way Out

In a true ethical dilemma, any choice you make will result in compromising a moral principle you believe in. You have to weigh which value is more important in that specific situation.

The Core Idea: A Moral Crossroads

An ethical dilemma forces you to choose between two conflicting moral paths.

Key Characteristics of a Dilemma

  • A Choice is Required: You cannot avoid making a decision. Inaction is often a decision in itself.
  • Multiple Options: There must be different courses of action you can take.
  • Moral Compromise: No matter what you choose, some ethical principle or value will be compromised.

Reading in Context: The Test

Imagine you are in an important exam. You look over and see your best friend, who has been struggling in class and is worried about failing, clearly cheating off a hidden notes sheet. The teacher is not looking, and you are the only one who sees it. You are now faced with an ethical dilemma.

Your moral values are in direct conflict. On one hand, you value honesty and fairness to all students who studied hard. This value tells you to report the cheating. On the other hand, you value loyalty to your friend, and you don't want to be the reason they get a zero and face serious consequences. If you tell the teacher, you betray your friend's trust. If you stay silent, you betray your own sense of right and wrong. There is no easy choice that doesn't involve a negative outcome and a compromise of your principles.
