Persuasion

Ethos, Pathos, and Logos

Breaking Down the Terms

Ethos

Greek for "Character."

Pathos

Greek for "Suffering" or "Experience."

Logos

Greek for "Word" or "Reason."

These are the three "modes of persuasion" identified by the philosopher Aristotle over 2,300 years ago.

The Core Idea: The Rhetorical Triangle

To persuade someone effectively, you usually need a mix of three things: you need them to trust you (Ethos), you need them to feel something (Pathos), and you need your argument to make sense (Logos).

The Three Appeals

1. Ethos (Credibility)

Focuses on the Speaker. Can the audience trust you? Are you an expert? Are you fair?

Example: "As a doctor with 20 years of experience, I recommend this treatment."

2. Pathos (Emotion)

Focuses on the Audience. Making them feel emotions like fear, pity, love, or anger to motivate them to act.

Example: "If we don't act now, these innocent puppies will have no home."

3. Logos (Logic)

Focuses on the Message. Using facts, statistics, historical evidence, and clear reasoning.

Example: "It costs 50% less to operate and saves $200 a year in energy."

Reading in Context: The Podcast Episode

Maya and Leo are recording a podcast episode about the local animal shelter. They want to persuade their teenage listeners to volunteer their time on weekends. To make an excellent episode, they strategically use all three appeals.

  • Ethos (Building Trust): They begin by interviewing the shelter director, an expert in animal care, and mention that they have both been volunteering there for six months. This shows the audience they know what they are talking about.
  • Pathos (Engaging Emotion): They play audio clips of happy dogs barking and tell the heartwarming story of "Barnaby," an old cat who finally found a forever home after years of waiting. This creates an emotional bond with the audience.
  • Logos (Making Sense): They end with facts: "The shelter has 50 dogs but only 10 walkers. If 10 listeners sign up for just one hour a week, every dog gets walked." This provides a logical, practical solution that is hard to argue against.
