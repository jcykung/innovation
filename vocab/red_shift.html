Red Shift

Breaking Down the Term

Red+Shift

Red: Refers to the color red at one end of the visible light spectrum. Red light has the longest wavelength we can see.

Shift: Means a change or movement in position.

So, "Red Shift" literally means the light from an object has moved or shifted towards the red end of the spectrum.

Concept #1: Light is a Wave

Light travels in waves. The distance between the peaks of these waves is called the wavelength. Different colors have different wavelengths. Blue and violet light have short wavelengths, while red light has long wavelengths.

The Visible Spectrum

Concept #2: The Doppler Effect (with Sound)

You've experienced this with sound. Imagine an ambulance siren. As it comes towards you, the sound waves get compressed, making the pitch higher. As it moves away, the sound waves stretch out, making the pitch lower. This stretching and compressing of waves from a moving source is the Doppler Effect.

The Core Idea: Redshift in Space

The Doppler Effect also applies to light! When a star or galaxy moves away from us, its light waves stretch out. This stretching shifts the light towards the longer-wavelength (red) end of the spectrum. This is Redshift. If it were moving towards us, it would be "Blueshifted."

What Redshift Tells Us

Astronomer Edwin Hubble observed that almost all distant galaxies show a redshift. The farther away a galaxy is, the more redshifted its light is. This leads to two huge conclusions:

1. The Universe is Expanding

If everything is moving away from everything else, the entire universe must be expanding, like the surface of an inflating balloon.

2. Evidence for the Big Bang

If the universe is expanding now, we can rewind time to a point where everything was packed into a single, hot, dense point. This is the core idea of the Big Bang theory.

Analogy: The Raisin Bread Universe

Imagine a lump of raisin bread dough before it's baked. You are on one raisin, and you see all the other raisins around you. As the dough bakes and expands, every raisin moves away from every other raisin. The raisins themselves aren't moving *through* the dough, but the dough itself is carrying them apart.

From your perspective on one raisin, a nearby raisin moves away slowly, but a raisin on the far side of the loaf moves away much faster. This is just like redshift. The galaxies are the raisins, and the expanding dough is spacetime. The farther away a galaxy (raisin) is, the faster it appears to move away from us, and the greater its light is redshifted.
