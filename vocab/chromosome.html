Chromosome

Breaking Down the Word: A Morphemic Analysis

chromo-+-some

Chromo-: From the Greek word "khrōma," meaning "colour."

-some: From the Greek word "sōma," meaning "body."

So, a "chromosome" is literally a "colour-body" because these structures absorb the colourful dyes scientists use to see them under a microscope.

The Core Idea: A Tightly Packed DNA Spool

Imagine trying to fit a 3-kilometre long thread into a tiny box. You'd have to wind it up incredibly tightly. That's a chromosome. It's a single, massive molecule of DNA that has been super-coiled and packaged around proteins to fit inside the microscopic nucleus of a cell. This organization keeps the DNA from getting tangled and allows it to be copied and moved accurately during cell division.

Chromosomes in Humans

Each human body cell contains a complete set of chromosomes, organized into pairs.

23 Pairs

Humans have 46 chromosomes in total, arranged in 23 pairs. You inherit one chromosome from each pair from your mother, and the other from your father. The first 22 pairs are called autosomes, and the 23rd pair are the sex chromosomes.

Sex Chromosomes (X and Y)

The 23rd pair determines biological sex. Females typically have two X chromosomes (XX), while males typically have one X and one Y chromosome (XY).

Reading in Context: A Karyotype Test

During a biology class, the teacher showed a picture called a karyotype. It was an organized image of all 46 human chromosomes from a single cell, arranged in their pairs from largest to smallest. "This map allows geneticists to check for abnormalities," the teacher explained. "For example, Down syndrome occurs when there's an extra copy of chromosome 21."

The teacher pointed to the last pair on the chart. "And by looking at the 23rd pair of chromosomes, we can also identify the biological sex of this individual. Since we see one X and one Y chromosome, we know this karyotype belongs to a male." The students could clearly see how these tiny structures contained a huge amount of information, serving as the blueprint for an entire person.
