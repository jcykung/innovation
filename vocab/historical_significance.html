Historical Significance

Breaking Down the Term

Historia+Significare

Historia: Greek for "inquiry" or "knowledge acquired by investigation."

Significare: Latin for "to show a sign" or "to matter."

So, "historical significance" is the process of deciding which past events matter enough to be investigated and remembered.

The Core Idea: The Great History Filter

The past is everything that ever happened to everyone, everywhere. History is the story we tell about the past. We cannot remember everything, so we use Historical Significance as a filter to decide what is important enough to study, write down, and teach.

How Do We Decide What is Significant?

Historians use specific criteria to determine if an event, person, or development is significant.

1. Prominence & Impact

Was the event recognized as important at the time? Did it affect a large number of people deeply?

2. Consequences

Did it result in dramatic or long-lasting change? Did it alter the course of the future?

3. Revealing

Does it help us understand a larger story? Does it shed light on issues (like racism or gender roles) that are important to us today?

Note: Significance varies over time. What was significant to people in 1867 might differ from what is significant to us today.

Reading in Context: Viola Desmond (1946)

In 1946, Viola Desmond, a Black businesswoman from Halifax, sat in the "whites-only" section of a movie theatre in New Glasgow, Nova Scotia. She was dragged out by police, jailed, and fined. At the time, this event was not "prominent" to most Canadians; it was a local legal dispute that many ignored.

However, today we view this event as having immense historical significance. Why? Because it is revealing: it shines a light on the history of segregation and racism in Canada, a topic that was often ignored in history books. It also had consequences: her courageous stand helped inspire the civil rights movement in Canada. Viola Desmond is now on the $10 bill, proving that significance is not just about wars and prime ministers; it is about events that tell us who we are as a nation.
