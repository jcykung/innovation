Punnett Square

Breaking Down the Name

Punnett+Square

Punnett: Named after the creator, British geneticist Reginald C. Punnett.

Square: Refers to the simple square grid that the tool uses.

So, a "Punnett Square" is simply a square diagram invented by Punnett.

The Core Idea: Predicting Genetic Possibilities

A Punnett square is a simple graphical tool used to predict all the possible genotypes of the offspring from a genetic cross. It shows the probability of a child inheriting a particular trait based on the genes of the parents.

Reading in Context: The Pea Plant Puzzle

A student has a tall pea plant (a dominant trait) but doesn't know if its genotype is homozygous dominant (TT) or heterozygous (Tt). To find out, they cross it with a short pea plant, which must have a homozygous recessive genotype (tt). They draw two possible Punnett squares to predict the outcomes.

The first Punnett square, assuming the tall parent is TT, shows that 100% of the offspring will be tall (genotype Tt). The second Punnett square, assuming the tall parent is Tt, predicts that 50% of the offspring will be tall (Tt) and 50% will be short (tt). When the student grows the seeds, about half of the new plants are short. They now have their answer: the parent plant must have been heterozygous (Tt).
